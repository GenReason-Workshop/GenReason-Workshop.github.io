About the Workshop

This workshop examines generation as a medium of cognition, asking whether and how generative models can reason across images, videos, and worlds. We span three levels of increasing visual and temporal complexity: images as a medium of thought, video generation for solving reasoning tasks, and world-grounded generation. Across this spectrum, we center on three questions: for which tasks is reasoning more naturally carried out visually than in language; how can models acquire reliable intermediate reasoning traces; and how should we evaluate such reasoning while distinguishing genuine inference from pattern approximation. We welcome contributions from image and video generation, world models, multimodal chain-of-thought, embodied agents, reinforcement learning, and benchmark design.

Topics of Interest

We invite submissions on the following topics and beyond.

Image-Level Generative Reasoning

  • Reasoning carried out through image generation and editing
  • Interleaved text-image chain-of-thought
  • Visual planning and spatial problem solving
  • Reasoning-informed image editing
  • Images as a medium of thought

Video-Level Generative Reasoning

  • Video generation for solving reasoning tasks
  • Next-frame prediction with physical and causal reasoning
  • Counterfactual and what-if rollouts
  • Chain-of-frames dynamics
  • Temporal consistency and physical plausibility

World-Level Generative Reasoning

  • World models and 3D / 4D generation
  • Unified multimodal models integrating world knowledge
  • Physical, spatial, causal, and procedural reasoning
  • World-state inference and simulation
  • Embodied decision-making and planning

Applications & Evaluation

  • Robotics planning and embodied simulation
  • Autonomous driving and scientific simulation
  • Measuring reasoning correctness vs. perceptual quality
  • Diagnosing physical, causal, and logical failures
  • Probing world knowledge and benchmarks
